Build a synthesis conclusion in 4 steps: Restate → Synthesize → Broader Significance → Clincher

Build Your Conclusion

1 · Restate
Restate your thesis in new words — don't copy it verbatim.
⚠️ Avoid starting with "In conclusion," "To sum up," "As I have shown," or "In this essay." These openers weaken your conclusion.
2 · Synthesize
Weave together your 2–3 main points — show how they connect, not just list them.
3 · Broader Significance
Zoom out — why does this argument matter beyond the essay?
4 · Clincher
A memorable final sentence — a call to action, a provocative question, or a resonant image.

What Makes a Strong Conclusion?

✅ Synthesizes — connects ideas, doesn't just list them
✅ Restates differently — thesis in new language
✅ Zooms out — broader implications for the world
✅ Ends memorably — leaves the reader thinking
❌ Never starts with "In conclusion" or "To sum up"
❌ Never introduces brand-new arguments
